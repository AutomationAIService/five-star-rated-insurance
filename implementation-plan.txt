1. Core Philosophy One Job: Capture a qualified lead and hand it to fulfillment. Every element on the site either reduces friction toward that goal or builds enough trust that the visitor is willing to engage. Zero-Form Approach: No traditional, static lead capture forms exist on this site.  The Guided Quote Assistant is the sole conversion engine. This is a deliberate architectural choice grounded in documented insurance-industry data showing ~5× conversion lift over static forms. Trust-First Brand: "Five Star Rated" is not just a name — it's the entire design system. Shield iconography, gold star accents, review density, and carrier logos all exist to answer the single most important visitor question: "Can I trust these people with my information?" Terminology Guardrails: Strictly enforced across all UI, copy, metadata, and support documentation. Allowed: "Guided Quote Assistant," "Quote Assistant," "Interactive Quote Builder," "Ava, your Quote Assistant".Forbidden: "AI bot," "automated chat," "chatbot," "virtual agent," "smart text," "automated assistant".Reasoning: Insurance shoppers are skeptical by default. The word "bot" introduces friction and signals "this isn't a real human interaction." The assistant must feel like a concierge. 2. Brand Identity & Design System Logo: Shield containing a prominent gold star, crowned by five smaller gold stars, on a deep navy blue background with gold accent borders. Wordmark: "FIVE STAR RATED" in navy, "INSURANCE" in gold beneath. Color Palette: Backed by financial-services color-psychology research. Primary: Navy Blue (#0A2A5E) - Headers, nav, primary headings, footer, user chat bubbles.Primary Dark: Midnight Navy (#051837) - Footer deepest areas, high-contrast text.Accent: Five Star Gold (#F5B800) - Stars, primary CTAs, accent lines, brand highlights.Background / Surface: Off-White (#FAFAF7) / Pure White (#FFFFFF).Usage Ratio: Navy ~60% / White ~30% / Gold ~10%. Gold scarcity is what keeps it feeling premium rather than cheap. All colors must be defined as custom values in tailwind.config.ts. Do NOT rely on default Tailwind blue-900 / yellow-500 — those will not match the logo. Typography: Plus Jakarta Sans (primary) or Inter (fallback), semi-bold/bold, H1 48–72px desktop. Body: Same family, regular weight. No serifs — keeps the modern, trustworthy fintech feel consistent. 3. Business Constraints (Confirmed) Carrier Partners: All logos display in the hero carrier strip (grayscale, hover-to-color) and throughout product landing pages: Allstate, Progressive, Geico, Farmers, Liberty Mutual, Safeco, Mercury, National General, Stillwater. Usage rights confirmed. Geographic Coverage — All 50 States: Five Star Rated Insurance accepts leads from all 50 U.S. states. No state exclusions. ZIP code is still captured at Step 1 of the assistant, but purely for rating and agent-routing purposes — not for exclusion gating. 4. TCPA Compliance (Non-Negotiable) Required Consent Step: Final step of the Guided Quote Assistant, before lead submission: Active, un-checked shadcn Checkbox component (pre-checked boxes are a TCPA violation in most jurisdictions). Explicit consent for automated calls/texts to the provided number. Consent for partner companies (Farmers, Progressive, Geico, etc.) to contact — links to /tcpa-consent partner list. Pre-Launch Legal Review: Before any paid traffic hits the site, have the following reviewed by a TCPA-specialized attorney: TCPA consent language, Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, Partner List page. Phase 2 Items: FCC 1-to-1 Consent Rule and State-Specific Mini-TCPA Laws (Florida FTSA, Washington WADFA, Oklahoma OTPA). 11-13.
